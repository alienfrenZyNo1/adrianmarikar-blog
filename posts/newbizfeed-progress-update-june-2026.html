NewBizFeed Progress Update: Fresh Company Leads, Fast

NewBizFeed launched earlier this month with a simple goal: turn public UK and Ireland company-register records into clean, useful, daily lead packs that sales teams, agencies, and freelancers can actually work with — without the noise, fake contacts, or opaque data sourcing that plagues most B2B lead tools.

This is a short progress update on where the product stands after its first few weeks.

The product is not a raw company register dump. It is a smaller, fresher, source-attributed list that has been filtered, categorised, and kept honest about what it does and does not contain.

What is live now

The core product is up and running at newbizfeed.com. You can browse curated lead packs organised by what you sell and where you sell it, preview the exact CSV structure for free, and download full exports as a subscriber. Every row carries an official source link so your team can verify it before outreach.

  • Fresh UK and Ireland coverage. Thousands of newly registered companies tracked across both registers, filtered for quality and usefulness.
  • Lead packs by industry and location. Start from a plain-English use case — web designers, accountants, IT support, telecoms, trades — instead of digging through SIC codes.
  • CSV exports with source links. Company name, number, incorporation date, industry, registered office, and relevance notes in every download.
  • Free sample downloads. Same columns as paid exports, up to 20 rows, no card required.
  • Compliance-first boundaries. No emails, no phone numbers, no opt-in claims. Just company-register intelligence with clear sourcing.

The site got a redesign

The public site went through a full visual refresh, including a new logo, a light-and-dark theme toggle, and a responsive polish pass across every page — homepage, pricing, lead packs, SEO landing pages, login, and account area. The goal was to make the product feel as clean and trustworthy as the data behind it.

Earlier posts on this blog covered the multi-agent review pipeline that processes the raw register data. That pipeline has since been hardened with better quality controls, honest progress reporting, and rate-limiting on public submissions so the numbers shown on the site always reflect reality.

Business owners can now suggest corrections

A new moderated listing-update feature is live. Business owners and representatives can suggest corrections to their own company records — contact details, website links, and similar fields. Every suggestion requires email confirmation and admin approval before anything changes publicly.

It is a small step, but an important one toward giving businesses control over how they appear in the product.

What is coming next

The roadmap is focused on three areas:

  1. Business owner data control. A full workflow for owners to claim their listing, verify their identity through domain-level checks, and manage corrections, suppressions, and opt-outs in a GDPR-safe way. This is the biggest piece of work ahead.
  2. Richer company signals. Better company-size estimation and enrichment so subscribers can filter leads more precisely — for example, separating solo traders from growing teams with real offices and budgets.
  3. Simpler pricing tiers. The lead-pack structure is being reviewed to make sure paid plans are clear and fair, whether you need a focused batch or broader monthly coverage.

Principles that will not change

A few things are baked into the product and will not shift as it grows:

  • Source links stay attached. Every record is traceable to an official register.
  • No unverifiable contact data. If it cannot be sourced from a public register, it does not get sold.
  • Responsible use is built in. Compliance notes travel with every CSV export.
  • The buying path stays simple. Browse samples, pick a plan, download CSVs. No demos, no calls, no pressure.

If you work in B2B sales, run an agency, or just want to know when fresh companies appear in your area, grab a free sample and see if the data fits your workflow.
