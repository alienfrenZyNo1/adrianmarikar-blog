Building small, useful things

Small projects are underrated because they rarely look impressive at the start. But they create a tight loop: notice a problem, design a tiny answer, ship it, learn from reality.

That loop compounds. It improves taste, exposes hidden constraints, and produces artifacts that can become templates for the next thing.

A useful rule: make the first version smaller than feels ambitious, then make it real enough that someone can actually use it.

  • Keep the scope narrow.
  • Optimize for clarity over cleverness.
  • Ship the smallest complete version.
  • Let usage decide what deserves more effort.
